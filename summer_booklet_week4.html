ROUNDING DECIMALS
Rounding decimals — same rule as whole numbers:
Look at the digit ONE place to the RIGHT of where you're rounding.
0–4 → round down,   5–9 → round up.
Round 4.376 to the nearest tenth: look at hundredths digit (7 ≥ 5) → round up → 4.4
Round 4.376 to the nearest hundredth: look at thousandths digit (6 ≥ 5) → round up → 4.38

MULTIPLYING DECIMALS
How to multiply decimals:
Step 1: Ignore the decimal points and multiply as whole numbers.
Step 2: Count total decimal places in BOTH numbers.
Step 3: Place the decimal point that many places from the RIGHT in your answer.
Example: 3.4 × 0.7 → 34 × 7 = 238 → 2 decimal places total → 2.38
Sense check: 3.4 × 0.7 ≈ 3 × 0.7 = 2.1. Answer 2.38 is close. ✓

DIVIDING DECIMALS
Dividing a decimal by a whole number: Place decimal in quotient directly above the decimal in the dividend. Divide as usual.
   6.48 ÷ 4 = 1.62   (think: 648 ÷ 4 = 162, then place decimal: 1.62)
Dividing by a decimal: Multiply BOTH numbers by 10/100 to make divisor a whole number.
   3.6 ÷ 0.4 = 36 ÷ 4 = 9   (multiply both by 10)
