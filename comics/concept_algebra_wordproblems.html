🧩 ALGEBRA WORD PROBLEMS! Define a variable · Write an equation · Solve · Check
Word problems are just
equations in disguise!
We need to translate
English → Math.
The 4-Step Method:
1 Define the variable
2 Write the equation
3 Solve it
4 Check your answer
The variable is like
the mystery box
we're trying to open! 📦
📖 Translating Words → Math Symbols
➕ Addition
sum, plus, more than,
increased by,
total, added to
➖ Subtraction
difference, minus,
less than, fewer,
decreased by, left
✖️ Multiplication
product, times,
twice (×2), triple (×3),
of, groups of
➗ Division
quotient, divided,
split equally,
shared, per, ratio
✅ Example 1: One-Step
"Maya has some stickers. She gives away 14 and has 23 left. How many did she start with?"
1 Let n = stickers Maya started with
2 Equation: n − 14 = 23
3 Solve: n = 23 + 14 = 37
4 Check: 37 − 14 = 23 ✓
✅ Example 2: One-Step ×
"Each box holds 8 crayons. There are 6 boxes. How many crayons total?"
1 Let c = total crayons
2 Equation: c = 8 × 6
3 Solve: c = 48
4 Check: 48 ÷ 6 = 8 ✓
Two-step problems need TWO operations — undo them in REVERSE ORDER!
Example 3 (Two-Step): "A book costs $5 more than twice the cost of a pen. The book costs $17. What does the pen cost?"
1 Let p = cost of pen
2 Equation: 2p + 5 = 17
3 Subtract 5: 2p = 12
     Divide by 2: p = 6
4 Check:
2 × 6 + 5 = 12 + 5 = 17 ✓

Pen costs $6
🏆 Example 4 (Multi-Step): Two Related Unknowns
"Zara is 3 times as old as Max. Together their ages add up to 28. How old is each?"
1 Let m = Max's age
     Then Zara = 3m
2 Equation: m + 3m = 28
3 Combine: 4m = 28
     Divide: m = 7
     So Zara = 3 × 7 = 21
4 Check:
7 + 21 = 28 ✓
21 = 3 × 7 ✓

Max = 7, Zara = 21
⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES
❌ "Less than" flips the order!
  "5 less than x" = x − 5 (not 5 − x)
❌ Not writing what the variable stands for
❌ Forgetting to check the answer
❌ Solving in wrong order for two-step
❌ Using the same variable for two different things
💡 PRO TIPS
✓ Underline key numbers in the problem
✓ Circle the operation words (more, less, times…)
✓ Write what x means before the equation
✓ For two unknowns, express both in terms of one variable
✓ Always check by substituting back in!
