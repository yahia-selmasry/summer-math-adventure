There must be a shorter way…
There IS a shortcut! We call it an EXPONENT!
Instead of writing 2 × 2 × 2, we write 23 — it means "three twos multiplied together"!
23
BASE
EXPONENT
= 2 × 2 × 2 = 8
BASE = the number being multiplied    EXPONENT = how many times to multiply it

Two special rules you MUST know!
Any number0 = 1

⚠️ WATCH OUT!
23 ≠ 2 × 3
23 means 2 × 2 × 2 = 8
NOT 2 × 3 = 6 ❌
The exponent is
how many times,
not a multiplier! 😤
