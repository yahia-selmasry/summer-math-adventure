🎲 Probability! How likely is something to happen?
Max, I have a bag with 3 red and 1 blue marble. What are the chances you pull out red?
🎒
4 marbles total
3 out of 4 are red… so I'd write 3/4? That's probability!
Exactly! The formula is always:
P(event) = favorable outcomes total outcomes
P(red) = 3/4
P(blue) = 1/4
P(green) = 0/4 = 0
The Probability Scale: 0 to 1
0
Impossible
½
Even
1
Certain
🚫 P = 0 → impossible (rolling a 7 on a 6-sided die)
🟡 P = ½ → equally likely (flipping heads)
✅ P = 1 → certain (sun rises tomorrow)
🎲 P = ¾ → likely (pulling red from our bag!)
Here's a cool shortcut: P(NOT happening) = 1 − P(happening)!
Example: P(rain) = 0.3
P(no rain) = 1 − 0.3 = 0.7
Bag example: P(NOT red) = 1 − 3/4 = 1/4
= P(blue) ✓ Makes sense!
Making Predictions:
Expected outcomes = P(event) × number of trials
Example: P(heads) = ½. Flip 40 times:
½ × 40 = 20 heads
This is a prediction — not guaranteed!
The more you try, the closer you get to the prediction! 📊
A spinner has 8 sections: 3 red, 2 blue, 2 yellow, 1 green. If you spin 40 times, expect how many reds?
P(red) = 3/8
3/8 × 40 = 15 reds
You might get 13 or 17 — that's okay! It's an estimate.
