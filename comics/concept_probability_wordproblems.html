🎲 PROBABILITY WORD PROBLEMS! Count outcomes · Write fraction · Simplify · Apply · Predict
Probability = favorable outcomes
÷ total outcomes!
Always a number
between 0 and 1.
The 5-Step Plan:
1 List or count ALL possible outcomes
2 Count FAVORABLE outcomes
3 Write P = favorable ÷ total
4 Simplify the fraction
5 Apply to predict outcomes
P(impossible) = 0
P(certain) = 1
Everything else is
between 0 and 1! 🎯
📖 Key Vocabulary & Probability Scale
🎯 Probability
P(event) = favorabletotal
0 = impossible
1 = certain
½ = equally likely
📋 Sample Space
All possible outcomes
Coin: {H, T}
Die: {1,2,3,4,5,6}
Card: {A-K, 4 suits}
🔮 Expected Outcomes
P × # trials = expected
P(heads)=½, 20 flips
→ ½ × 20 = 10 heads
(not guaranteed!)
🎲 Signal Words
"likely · unlikely · certain"
"what are the chances?"
"probability of picking"
"predict how many times"
✅ Example 1 — Basic Probability (Bag of Marbles)
"A bag has 6 red, 4 blue, and 2 green marbles. What is the probability of picking a red marble?"
1 Total outcomes: 6+4+2 = 12
2 Favorable (red): 6
3 P(red) = 612
4 Simplify: 612 = 12
5 50% chance — equally likely ✓
✅ Example 2 — Predicting Outcomes
"A spinner has 4 equal sections: red, blue, green, yellow. If you spin 40 times, how many times do you EXPECT blue?"
1 Total sections: 4
2 P(blue) = 14
4 Expected = P × trials
   = 14 × 40 = 10 times
5 Expected ≠ guaranteed!
   With many trials, results get closer ✓
✅ Example 3 — Sample Space (Two Events)
"You flip a coin AND roll a die. What is the probability of getting heads AND a 4?"
1 List sample space:
H1, H2, H3, H4, H5, H6
T1, T2, T3, T4, T5, T6
Total = 12 outcomes
2 Favorable: H4 = 1
3 P(H and 4) = 112
5 P = ½ × ⅙ = 112 ✓
(Multiply probabilities for AND)
Make a table or
tree diagram to
see ALL outcomes!
✅ Example 4 — Complementary Probability
"A deck of cards has 52 cards. You draw one. What is the probability of NOT getting a heart?"
1 Total cards: 52
Hearts: 13 (one suit)
2 P(heart) = 1352 = 14
3 P(NOT heart) = 1 − P(heart)
= 1 − 14 = 34
5 P(event) + P(not event) = 1 ✓
1/4 + 3/4 = 1 ✓
⚠️ CLASSIC MISTAKES
❌ Forgetting to count ALL total outcomes
❌ Thinking expected outcomes are guaranteed
❌ Not simplifying the probability fraction
❌ Confusing P(A and B) with P(A or B)
❌ Using "out of" backwards: favorable goes on TOP
❌ Forgetting P(event) + P(not event) = 1
💡 PRO TIPS
✓ P = favorabletotal — favorable always on TOP
✓ For two events (AND): multiply probabilities
✓ P(NOT event) = 1 − P(event)
✓ Predicted outcomes = P × total trials
✓ Draw a tree diagram or table for two events
✓ Probability is always between 0 and 1

🏆 Level Up — Multi-Step Probability Problems
Experiments don't
always match the
expected outcomes!
Example: "Max rolls a die 60 times and records the results: 1→8, 2→12, 3→9, 4→11, 5→10, 6→10. What was the experimental probability of rolling a 2? How does this compare to the theoretical probability?"
Experimental
Rolled 2: 12 times
Total rolls: 60
P(2) experimental = 1260 = 15
Theoretical
P(2) = 16 ≈ 0.167
Experimental = 15 = 0.20
Close, but not exact — that's normal!
