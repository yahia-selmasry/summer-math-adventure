📋 Geometry Formulas & Signal Words
📏 Perimeter
P = add ALL sides
Rectangle: P = 2(l + w)
Signal: "fencing · border · around · outline"
⬛ Area (Rectangle)
A = length × width
Units: ft², m², in²
Signal: "carpet · floor · tile · field"
🔷 Area (Triangle)
A = 12 × base × height
Units: ft², m², in²
Signal: "triangular garden"
📦 Volume (Prism)
V = l × w × h
Units: ft³, m³, in³
Signal: "box · tank · room"
✅ Example 1 — Perimeter (Fencing)
"Zara wants to fence her rectangular garden. It is 15 ft long and 9 ft wide. How many feet of fencing does she need?"
1 Draw a rectangle: 15 ft × 9 ft
2 Label sides: 15, 9, 15, 9
3 Formula: P = 2(l + w)
4 P = 2(15 + 9) = 2(24) = 48 ft
5 No units² — perimeter is just feet ✓
✅ Example 2 — Area (Carpet)
"A classroom is 28 ft long and 22 ft wide. Carpet costs $3 per sq ft. What is the total cost?"
1 Draw: 28 ft × 22 ft room
3 Formula: A = l × w
4 A = 28 × 22 = 616 ft²
   Cost = 616 × $3 = $1,848
5 Area needs ft² label ✓
   Estimate: 30×20=600 ft², close!
✅ Example 3 — Volume of a Rectangular Prism
"A fish tank is 3 ft long, 2 ft wide, and 1.5 ft tall. How many cubic feet of water does it hold?"
1 Draw a 3D box: 3 × 2 × 1.5
3 Formula: V = l × w × h
4 V = 3 × 2 × 1.5
= 3 × 2 = 6
6 × 1.5 = 9 ft³
5 Units = ft³ (cubic) ✓
Estimate: 3×2×1 = 6, close!
Volume uses 3 measurements:
length × width × height!
✅ Example 4 — Combined / Irregular Area
"Max's bedroom is an L-shape: one section is 10 ft × 8 ft and the other is 6 ft × 4 ft. What is the total area?"
Trick Split the L into 2 rectangles
Rectangle 1: 10 × 8 = 80 ft²
Rectangle 2: 6 × 4 = 24 ft²
Total Area = 80 + 24 = 104 ft²
5 Always label area in ft² ✓
Could also subtract a missing piece method!
⚠️ CLASSIC MISTAKES
❌ Using area formula for perimeter problems (and vice versa!)
❌ Forgetting to label units: ft² for area, ft³ for volume
❌ Only multiplying two sides for volume (forgetting height!)
❌ Not drawing the shape before calculating
❌ Adding all sides wrong when one side isn't labeled
❌ Forgetting to double (×2) sides in the perimeter formula
💡 PRO TIPS
✓ DRAW the shape FIRST — label every side you know
✓ Perimeter = going AROUND (add all sides)
✓ Area = FILLING inside (multiply l × w)
✓ Volume = 3D box (l × w × h)
✓ For L-shapes: break into 2 rectangles, then add areas
✓ Always label: ft for P, ft² for A, ft³ for V

🏆 Level Up — Multi-Step Geometry Problems
Sometimes you need
area AND perimeter
in the same problem!
Example: "Zara has a pool that is 8 m long and 5 m wide. She wants to put a fence around it and fill it with water to a depth of 2 m. Find: (a) perimeter for fencing, (b) volume of water needed."
Perimeter
P = 2(8 + 5) = 2(13)
= 26 m of fencing
Volume
V = 8 × 5 × 2
= 80 m³ of water
