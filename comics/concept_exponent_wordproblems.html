📖 Key Vocabulary & Quick Reference
⚡ Exponents
34 = 3×3×3×3 = 81
Base = 3; Power = 4
"cubed" = to the 3rd power
"squared" = to the 2nd power
🔟 Powers of 10
101 = 10
102 = 100
103 = 1,000
106 = 1,000,000
⬜ Perfect Squares
1² = 1, 2² = 4, 3² = 9
4² = 16, 5² = 25, 6² = 36
7² = 49, 8² = 64, 9² = 81
10² = 100, 12² = 144

"squared · cubed · power of"

✅ Example 1 — Powers of 10 in Context
"A science book says bacteria can double to 106 in one hour. How many bacteria is that?"
1 Base = 10; Exponent = 6
2 Expand: 10×10×10×10×10×10
3 Shortcut: 106 = 1 with 6 zeros
4 Answer: 1,000,000 bacteria
5 One million bacteria — that's a LOT! ✓
✅ Example 2 — Perfect Squares (Area)
"Max's square garden has sides of 9 feet. What is the area? Is 81 a perfect square?"
1 Side length = 9 ft
3 Area = side² = 9²
4 9² = 9 × 9 = 81 ft²
   Yes! 81 is a perfect square (9 × 9)
5 Perfect squares always come from
   a whole number × itself ✓
✅ Example 3 — Exponential Doubling
"A chain letter: you send it to 2 friends. Each friend sends it to 2 more. After 4 rounds, how many people got the letter in that last round?"
1 Each round = × 2
Round 1: 21 = 2
Round 2: 22 = 4
Round 3: 23 = 8
Round 4: 24 = ?
24 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2
= 4 × 4 = 16 people
5 Makes sense: doubles
each round: 2→4→8→16 ✓
Doubling = base 2!
Tripling = base 3!
Each round = +1
to the exponent!
✅ Example 4 — Powers of 10 & Place Value
"A computer has 4 × 104 files. A server has 3 × 106 files. How many more files does the server have?"
1 Computer: 4 × 104
104 = 10,000
4 × 10,000 = 40,000 files

2 Server: 3 × 106
106 = 1,000,000
3 × 1,000,000 = 3,000,000 files
4 Difference:
3,000,000 − 40,000
= 2,960,000 more files
5 Server has about 75× more ✓
⚠️ CLASSIC MISTAKES
❌ Multiplying the base by the exponent: 2³ ≠ 6 (it's 8!)
❌ Thinking 10⁵ = 50 (count the ZEROS: 10⁵ = 100,000)
❌ Forgetting to write the units (ft², not just the number)
❌ Confusing "squared" (×2) with "to the power of 2" (×itself)
❌ Not expanding step by step for larger exponents
❌ Thinking any square number is a perfect square (technically all are!)
💡 PRO TIPS
✓ Exponent = HOW MANY TIMES to multiply the base by itself
✓ Powers of 10: count the zeros = the exponent
✓ Perfect squares: memorize 1–12 squared
✓ For doubling problems: each step = exponent increases by 1
✓ Scientific notation: number × 10^n means move decimal n places right
✓ Any number to the 0th power = 1 (bonus fact!)

Three cubes
