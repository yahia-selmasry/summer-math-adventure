US CUSTOMARY — LENGTH
12 inches (in)=1 foot (ft)
3 feet (ft)=1 yard (yd)
36 inches=1 yard
5,280 feet=1 mile (mi)
1,760 yards=1 mile
Trick: 12 in = 1 ft → "A foot has 12 toes... wait, that's not right. A ruler has 12 inches!" 📏
US CUSTOMARY — WEIGHT
16 ounces (oz)=1 pound (lb)
2,000 pounds (lb)=1 ton (T)

Real-world sense:
A slice of bread ≈ 1 oz
A football ≈ 1 lb
A small car ≈ 1 ton
Trick: "16 oz = 1 lb" → Sweet 16 pounds! 🎂
US CUSTOMARY — CAPACITY
8 fl oz=1 cup (c)
2 cups=1 pint (pt)
2 pints=1 quart (qt)
4 quarts=1 gallon (gal)
Trick: "Gallon Man" — a Gallon holds 4 Quarts, 8 Pints, or 16 Cups! 🥛

Converting US Customary — The Golden Rule:
To convert to a SMALLER unit → MULTIPLY
(You need more of the smaller pieces)
Example: 3 ft × 12 = 36 inches

To convert to a LARGER unit → DIVIDE
(Fewer of the bigger pieces)
Example: 48 oz ÷ 16 = 3 pounds
Smaller unit?
Multiply!
Bigger unit?
Divide!

METRIC — LENGTH
10 mm=1 cm
100 cm=1 m
1,000 m=1 km
Real-world sense:
A fingernail ≈ 1 cm wide
A doorway ≈ 2 m tall
Walking 1 km ≈ 10 min
METRIC — MASS
1,000 mg=1 g
1,000 g=1 kg
Real-world sense:
A paper clip ≈ 1 g
A math textbook ≈ 1 kg
A bag of apples ≈ 3 kg
Note: In science we say "mass," not "weight." A scale on Earth measures weight, but kg measures mass!
METRIC — CAPACITY
1,000 mL=1 L
Real-world sense:
A teaspoon ≈ 5 mL
A water bottle ≈ 500 mL
A big soda bottle = 2 L
1 L is a little more than 4 cups (US)! 🥤
Worked Examples — Converting Within the Metric System
Example 1 — cm to mm (smaller unit)
"A beetle is 4.5 cm long. How many mm is that?"
Think cm → mm = smaller, so MULTIPLY
Fact 1 cm = 10 mm
Solve 4.5 × 10 = 45 mm
Example 2 — g to kg (larger unit)
"A science experiment uses 2,500 g of sand. How many kg is that?"
Think g → kg = larger, so DIVIDE
Fact 1,000 g = 1 kg
Solve 2,500 ÷ 1,000 = 2.5 kg
Example 3 — m to km (larger unit)
"A road trip is 3,500 m. How many km?"
Think m → km = larger, so DIVIDE
Fact 1,000 m = 1 km
Solve 3,500 ÷ 1,000 = 3.5 km
Example 4 — mL to L (larger unit)
"You pour 750 mL into a jug. How many liters?"
Think mL → L = larger, so DIVIDE
Fact 1,000 mL = 1 L
Solve 750 ÷ 1,000 = 0.75 L
Word Problems with US Customary Conversions
Example A — Cooking: "A recipe needs 3 cups of milk. You have a 1-quart container that is full. Is that enough milk?"
Step 1 Convert quart to cups: 1 qt = 2 pt = 4 cups
Step 2 Compare: 4 cups available vs. 3 cups needed
Answer 4 > 3, so YES, it is enough! You'll have 1 cup left over.
Example B — Sports: "A football field is 100 yards long. Max ran across it 4 times. How many feet did he run?"
Step 1 Total yards: 100 × 4 = 400 yards
Step 2 Convert yards → feet (smaller unit, multiply)
   400 yd × 3 = 1,200 feet
Check 1,200 ÷ 3 = 400 yd ✓
Perimeter & Area with Unit Conversions — Real-World Twist!
Always use the
same units before

or perimeter!
Example C: "A garden is 2 yards wide and 5 feet long. What is the area in square feet?"
Step 1 Convert to same unit
2 yards × 3 = 6 feet
Step 2 Calculate area
Area = length × width
Area = 5 ft × 6 ft
= 30 square feet
Check Both in feet ✓
Units match → answer is correct!
TIME CONVERSIONS
KEY TIME FACTS
60 seconds=1 minute
60 minutes=1 hour
24 hours=1 day
7 days=1 week
52 weeks=1 year
365 days=1 year
Trick: "60 and 60 and 24" — seconds, minutes, hours! ⏱️
Elapsed Time Example
"Zara started reading at 2:45 PM and stopped at 4:10 PM. How long did she read?"
Method Count up from 2:45 PM
2:45 → 3:00 = 15 minutes
3:00 → 4:00 = 60 minutes
4:00 → 4:10 = 10 minutes
Total: 15 + 60 + 10 = 85 minutes = 1 hr 25 min
CLASSIC MISTAKES
Converting to bigger unit but multiplying instead of dividing
Mixing units (adding feet and yards without converting)
Forgetting to include units in your answer
Confusing ounces (weight) with fluid ounces (volume)
Using mm = cm instead of 10 mm = 1 cm
Not converting before calculating area or perimeter
PRO TIPS
Smaller unit? Multiply (you need MORE pieces)
Bigger unit? Divide (you need FEWER pieces)
Write the conversion fact FIRST before solving
Always label your units at every step
Check: does your answer make real-world sense?
Draw a number line for elapsed time problems!
