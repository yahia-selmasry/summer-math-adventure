📦 VOLUME = Space inside
Volume = how many unit cubes
fit inside the box!
VOLUME formula: V = l × w × h
Example: l = 5 cm, w = 3 cm, h = 2 cm
V = 5 × 3 × 2 = 30 cm³
🎁 Layer thinking!
V = (l × w) × h = base area × height
🎨 SURFACE AREA = The wrapping paper!
Surface area = total area of ALL 6 faces added together.
SURFACE AREA formula: SA = 2(lw) + 2(lh) + 2(wh)
Face pairArea each×2
Top + Bottoml × w2lw
Front + Backl × h2lh
Left + Rightw × h2wh
🔢 Let's work one together! A box: l=6, w=4, h=3 (all in cm)
Step 1 – VOLUME
V = l × w × h
V = 6 × 4 × 3
V = 24 × 3
V = 72 cm³
Step 2 – SURFACE AREA
2(lw) = 2(6×4) = 2(24) = 48
2(lh) = 2(6×3) = 2(18) = 36
2(wh) = 2(4×3) = 2(12) = 24
SA = 48 + 36 + 24 = 108 cm²
Volume uses cm³ (cubed).
Surface Area uses cm² (squared).
Don't mix them up!
⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES!
❌ Volume ≠ Surface Area
V = 72 cm³ measures inside space
SA = 108 cm² measures outside skin

❌ Forget to multiply by 2
Each face has a PAIR (top&bottom, etc.)

❌ Wrong units
Volume → cubic units (cm³, in³)
Area → square units (cm², in²)
🏠 Real World Uses
📦 Shipping box: need volume to know how much fits inside

🎁 Wrapping gift: need surface area to know how much paper

🏊 Fish tank: volume = how much water

🖼️ Painting walls: surface area = how much paint

📐 NET = Unfolded box (flat pattern)
If you cut open a box and flatten it out,
you get a net. The net shows all 6 faces.
Adding up all the face areas = Surface Area!

SA = sum of all face areas on the net
Fold the net back up → it makes the 3D box again! ✨
