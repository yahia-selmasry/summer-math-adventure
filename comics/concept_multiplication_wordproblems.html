2 Draw an array or groups
3 Choose × or ÷
4 Calculate; find remainder
5 Does the remainder make sense?

🔍 Which Operation? Key Signal Words
✖️ MULTIPLY when...
"each · per · every"
"3 rows of 24 seats"
"12 boxes with 8 each"
"repeated groups"
➗ DIVIDE when...
"share equally · split"
"how many groups?"
"how many in each?"
"cut into equal pieces"
🔢 DIVISIBILITY
Divisible by 2? → even
Divisible by 3? → digit sum ÷ 3
Divisible by 5? → ends in 0/5
Divisible by 10? → ends in 0
✅ Example 1 — Multi-digit Multiplication
"A movie theater has 24 rows of seats with 36 seats per row. How many seats total?"
1 Groups: 24 rows; Size: 36 seats
2 Draw: 24 rows × 36 columns array
3 Operation: MULTIPLY
4 24 × 36:
   24 × 30 = 720
   24 × 6 = 144
   720 + 144 = 864 seats
5 Estimate: 25×36 = 900, close! ✓
✅ Example 2 — Division with Remainder
"Max has 157 stickers to share equally among 6 friends. How many does each get? Any left over?"
1 Total: 157; Groups: 6
3 Operation: DIVIDE
4 157 ÷ 6:
   6 × 26 = 156
   157 − 156 = 1 remainder
   Each friend gets 26 stickers
   Remainder: 1 sticker left
5 Check: 6 × 26 + 1 = 157 ✓
✅ Example 3 — What do you DO with the remainder?
"Zara needs to pack 85 cupcakes into boxes of 12. How many full boxes? Does she need one more box?"
1 Total: 85; Per box: 12
3 DIVIDE: 85 ÷ 12
4 12 × 7 = 84
   85 − 84 = 1 leftover
7 full boxes + 1 extra cupcake
→ She needs 8 boxes total!
5 ROUND UP the remainder
when you can't leave
cupcakes out! ✓
Remainder rules:
Drop it · Round up ·
Use it AS the answer!
✅ Example 4 — Divisibility in Context
"A coach has 144 players. Can they be split into equal teams of 6? Of 8? Of 7?"
Teams of 6?
144 ÷ 6 = 24
No remainder! ✓
YES — 24 teams
Teams of 8?
144 ÷ 8 = 18
No remainder! ✓
YES — 18 teams
Teams of 7?
144 ÷ 7 = 20 R4
Remainder 4 ✗
NO — doesn't work!
⚠️ CLASSIC MISTAKES
❌ Adding when you should multiply ("3 boxes, 8 each" → should be ×)
❌ Forgetting to write the remainder in division
❌ Always dropping the remainder (sometimes you round UP!)
❌ Not checking divisibility before dividing
❌ Multiplying the wrong two numbers in a word problem
❌ Forgetting units in the answer
💡 PRO TIPS
✓ "Each / per / every" → look for the rate → MULTIPLY
✓ "Share equally / split / how many groups?" → DIVIDE
✓ Always check: does the remainder make sense for this problem?
✓ Use partial products: break big numbers into tens + ones
✓ Check division with multiplication: quotient × divisor + R = dividend
✓ Estimate first: round to nearest 10s, then multiply
