A variable is just a
mystery number!
Let n = the unknown,
then write an equation.
The 5-Step Plan:
1 Read & find the unknown
2 Let n = (what you're finding)
3 Write the equation
4 Solve for n
5 Check by substituting back
An inequality uses
<, >, ≤, or ≥
instead of = !

🔍 Key Vocabulary & Signal Words
➕ Addition
"more than · increased by"
"sum · total · combined"
"plus · added to"
→ n + a = b
➖ Subtraction
"less than · decreased by"
"difference · fewer"
"minus · taken away"
→ n − a = b
✖️ Multiplication
"times · product · double"
"triple · per · each"
"groups of · rate"
→ a × n = b
⚖️ Inequalities
"at least · minimum"→ ≥
"at most · maximum"→ ≤
"more than"→ >
"fewer than"→ <
✅ Example 1 — Write & Solve an Equation
"Zara has some trading cards. She gives away 17 and has 43 left. How many did she start with?"
1 Unknown: starting cards
2 Let n = starting cards
3 Equation: n − 17 = 43
4 Add 17 to both sides:
   n = 43 + 17 = 60 cards
5 Check: 60 − 17 = 43 ✓
✅ Example 2 — Multiplication Equation
"Max packs books into boxes of equal size. He fills 8 boxes and has 136 books. How many per box?"
1 Unknown: books per box
2 Let n = books per box
3 Equation: 8n = 136
4 Divide both sides by 8:
   n = 136 ÷ 8 = 17 books
5 Check: 8 × 17 = 136 ✓
✅ Example 3 — Writing an Inequality
"A roller coaster requires riders to be at least 48 inches tall. Zara is 46 inches. Does she qualify? Write an inequality."
1 Requirement: h ≥ 48 in
2 Zara's height: h = 46
3 Inequality: h ≥ 48
4 Is 46 ≥ 48? NO
Zara does NOT qualify.

She needs to grow at least:
48 − 46 = 2 more inches
5 46 < 48 confirms she's too short ✓
"At least" means
that number OR
bigger → use ≥ !
✅ Example 4 — Evaluating an Expression
"A pizza shop charges $8 plus $1.50 per topping. Write an expression for the total cost. How much for 5 toppings?"
2 Let t = number of toppings
3 Expression: 8 + 1.50t
4 Plug in t = 5:
Cost = 8 + 1.50(5)
= 8 + 7.50
= $15.50
5 Makes sense: $8 base + $7.50 for toppings ✓
⚠️ CLASSIC MISTAKES
❌ "Less than" means the number goes FIRST: "5 less than n" = n − 5, not 5 − n
❌ Not performing the same operation to BOTH sides
❌ Confusing ≥ (at least) with > (more than)
❌ Forgetting to check your answer by substituting back
❌ Not defining your variable clearly (let n = ?)
❌ Writing an expression instead of an equation (missing the = sign)
💡 PRO TIPS
✓ Always start: "Let n = ___" to define your variable
✓ Key moves: add/subtract/multiply/divide BOTH sides equally
✓ Expressions have no = sign; equations do
✓ "At least" → ≥ · "At most" → ≤ · "More than" → > · "Less than" → <
✓ Always check: plug your answer back into the original equation
✓ Label your answer with proper units!
