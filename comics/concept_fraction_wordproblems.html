🍕 FRACTION WORD PROBLEMS! Read it · Draw it · Set it up · Solve it · Check it
The secret to fraction
word problems? Always ask:
"Fraction OF what?"
That tells you what to multiply!
The 5-Step Plan:
1 Read & underline numbers
2 Draw a picture or bar
3 Choose + − × ÷
4 Calculate carefully
5 Check: does it make sense?

🔍 Which Operation Do I Use?
➕ ADD fractions when...
You're combining parts:
"ate 1/3 and then 1/4 more"
"total eaten altogether"
➖ SUBTRACT fractions when...
You're finding what's left:
"had 3/4, used 1/3"
"how much remains?"
✖️ MULTIPLY fractions when...
You see "of":
"2/3 of 12 students"
"1/2 of 41/2 cups"
➗ DIVIDE fractions when...
You're splitting into groups:
"share 3/4 pizza among 3"
"how many 1/2s fit in 6?"
✅ Example 1 — ADD (different denominators)
"Zara ate 13 of a pizza at lunch and 14 at dinner. How much did she eat in all?"
1 Underline: ⅓ + ¼
2 Draw: 2 fraction bars
3 Operation: ADD (combining)
4 LCD = 12
   13 = 412    14 = 312
   412 + 312 = 712
5 Makes sense? ⅓ ≈ 0.33, ¼ = 0.25, sum ≈ 0.58 ✓
✅ Example 2 — MULTIPLY ("of" a whole)
"A bag has 24 marbles. 38 of them are red. How many red marbles?"
1 Underline: ³⁄₈ of 24
2 Draw: 24 dots in 8 groups
3 "of" = MULTIPLY
4 38 × 24 = 3 × 248 = 728 = 9
5 Check: 24 ÷ 8 = 3 per group
   3 groups × 3 = 9 ✓
✅ Example 3 — SUBTRACT (finding what's left)
"Max had 56 of a cake. He ate 14 of the whole cake. How much is left?"
1 Underline: ⁵⁄₆ − ¼
3 Operation: SUBTRACT
4 LCD of 6 and 4 = 12
   56 = 1012   14 = 312
1012 − 312 = 712
5 Makes sense?
⁵⁄₆ ≈ 0.83, ate 0.25
0.83 − 0.25 ≈ 0.58 ≈ ⁷⁄₁₂ ✓
Subtract = "what's
left over" or
"how much more?"
✅ Example 4 — Mixed Numbers ("of" with a mixed number)
"A hiking trail is 412 miles long. Zara walked 23 of the trail. How far did she walk?"
1 Underline: ⅔ of 4½
2 4½ miles → draw a bar divided into thirds
3 "of" = MULTIPLY
4 Convert: 4½ = 92
   23 × 92 = 186 = 3 miles
5 Check: ⅔ of ~4.5
≈ 0.67 × 4.5 ≈ 3.0 ✓
She walked 3 miles!
⚠️ CLASSIC MISTAKES
❌ Adding the denominators: ¼ + ½ = ²⁄₆ ✗
❌ Forgetting to convert mixed numbers before multiplying
❌ Not finding the LCD before adding/subtracting
❌ Multiplying when you should be adding (missing the key word)
❌ Not simplifying the final answer
❌ Forgetting the units (miles, cups, pieces…)
💡 PRO TIPS
✓ Circle "of" → it always means multiply
✓ "Altogether / total / in all" → ADD
✓ "Left / remain / difference" → SUBTRACT
✓ Always convert mixed numbers to improper fractions first
✓ Draw a bar model — it's not cheating, it's smart!
✓ Estimate first: does your answer make sense?

🏆 Level Up — Multi-Step Fraction Word Problems
Multi-step = do it
one piece at a time!
Example: "A class of 30 students: 35 chose soccer, 16 chose swimming. The rest chose basketball. How many chose each sport?"
Soccer
35 × 30 = 905 = 18 students

Swimming
16 × 30 = 306 = 5 students
Basketball
30 − 18 − 5 = 7 students

Check
18 + 5 + 7 = 30 ✓
All students accounted for!
