📊 STATISTICS WORD PROBLEMS! Sort data · Find mean, median, mode, range · Interpret · Compare
Mean = fair share average
Median = middle number
Mode = most frequent
Range = spread (max−min)
The 5-Step Plan:
1 Write the data in ORDER (low to high)
2 Find what's asked: mean/median/mode/range
3 Apply the correct formula
4 Calculate carefully
5 Interpret: what does it tell us?

📋 Formulas & Memory Tricks
📊 MEAN (Average)
Mean = Sum ÷ Count
Add ALL values, then
divide by HOW MANY
"fair share"
🏅 MEDIAN
Sort → find MIDDLE
Odd count: middle value
Even count: average of
the 2 middle values
🔁 MODE
Most FREQUENT value
Can have 0, 1, or
more than 1 mode
"MOde = MOst"
📏 RANGE
Range = Max − Min
Shows how SPREAD OUT
the data is
large range = varied data
✅ Example 1 — All Four Measures
"Zara's quiz scores: 85, 92, 78, 92, 88, 75, 90. Find the mean, median, mode, and range."
1 Sort: 75, 78, 85, 88, 90, 92, 92
Mean Sum = 75+78+85+88+90+92+92 = 600
   600 ÷ 7 = 85.7 (≈ 86)
Median Middle (4th value) = 88
Mode 92 appears twice → 92
Range 92 − 75 = 17
✅ Example 2 — Even Number of Values
"Max recorded daily steps: 8,200 · 9,500 · 7,800 · 10,100 · 8,600 · 9,200. Find the mean and median."
1 Sort: 7800, 8200, 8600, 9200, 9500, 10100
Mean Sum = 53,400
   53,400 ÷ 6 = 8,900 steps
Median Two middle values: 8600 & 9200
   (8600 + 9200) ÷ 2 = 17800 ÷ 2 = 8,900
✅ Example 3 — Interpreting & Comparing Data Sets
"Team A points: 12, 18, 14, 20, 11. Team B points: 15, 16, 14, 15, 15. Which team is more consistent? Which scored higher on average?"
Team A:
Sort: 11, 12, 14, 18, 20
Mean = 75 ÷ 5 = 15
Range = 20 − 11 = 9
Median = 14
Team B:
Sort: 14, 15, 15, 15, 16
Mean = 75 ÷ 5 = 15
Range = 16 − 14 = 2
Median = Mode = 15
Team B is more consistent (smaller range)!
Same mean but
different range means
one team is more
consistent!
✅ Example 4 — Effect of an Outlier on the Mean
"Students' book counts: 8, 7, 9, 6, 8, 50. Find the mean with and without the outlier (50). Which better represents the data?"
With outlier
Sum = 8+7+9+6+8+50 = 88
Mean = 88 ÷ 6 = 14.67
Pulled way up by the 50!
Without outlier
Sum = 8+7+9+6+8 = 38
Mean = 38 ÷ 5 = 7.6 books
This better represents most students!
→ Use median when outliers exist ✓
⚠️ CLASSIC MISTAKES
❌ Forgetting to SORT before finding median
❌ Confusing mean and median (they're different!)
❌ Finding the wrong middle value for even-count data sets
❌ Saying "no mode" when all values appear once (say "no mode")
❌ Subtracting mean − min for range (it's max − min!)
❌ Not including units in the final answer
💡 PRO TIPS
✓ ALWAYS sort data first — it unlocks median and mode instantly
✓ Mean: add everything, then divide by the COUNT (not the sum!)
✓ Even count → average the two middle values for median
✓ Outliers pull the MEAN but don't affect the median much
✓ When comparing data sets, use RANGE to measure spread
✓ Mode is great for finding the "most popular" item

🏆 Level Up — Multi-Step Statistics Problems
Use the mean to
find a missing value
in a data set!
Example: "Zara took 5 math tests. Her scores were 88, 92, 79, and 95. She doesn't know her 5th score yet. If her goal is an average of 90, what does she need on the 5th test?"
Target mean
Goal: mean = 90
5 tests total
Needed total sum = 90 × 5 = 450
Find missing score
Current sum = 88+92+79+95 = 354
5th score needed:
450 − 354 = 96
She needs a 96 on the 5th test!
