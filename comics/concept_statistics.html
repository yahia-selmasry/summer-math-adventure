🟢 MEAN = Average
Add ALL the values, then divide by HOW MANY there are!
Data: 7, 9, 5, 9, 10
Step 1 Add: 7 + 9 + 5 + 9 + 10 = 40
Step 2 Count: 5 values
Step 3 Divide: 40 ÷ 5 = 8
Mean = Sum ÷ Count
Think of it as "evening out" all values so they're all equal.
⚠️ Mean Watch-outs
• One really big or small number can pull the mean away from center
• Example: scores 5, 6, 6, 7, 100
  Mean = 124 ÷ 5 = 24.8 — is that really "typical"?
• This is why we also use median!
🔵 MEDIAN = Middle Value
First put ALL values in ORDER from least to greatest. Then find the middle one!
ODD count → 1 middle number
Data: 7, 9, 5, 9, 10
Sort 5, 7, 9, 9, 10
Median = 9
EVEN count → average the 2 middle
Data: 4, 7, 9, 12
Middle two 7, 9
Median = (7 + 9) ÷ 2 = 8
🟠 MODE = Most Frequent
The value that appears most often.
Data: 7, 9, 5, 9, 10 → Mode = 9 (appears twice)

Special cases:
• No mode — all values appear once
• Two modes (bimodal) — two values tied for most
• Many modes — multiple values tied
Memory trick: Mode sounds like "most" — the most popular value!
🟣 RANGE = Spread
Range = Largest − Smallest

Data: 7, 9, 5, 9, 10
Largest = 10   Smallest = 5
Range = 10 − 5 = 5

Range tells you how spread out the data is.
Small range → data is close together
Large range → data is spread apart

🤔 When to Use Which?
Mean — balanced data, no outliers
(test scores in a normal class)
Median — when there's an outlier
(house prices — one mansion skews the mean)
Mode — most popular item
(shoe sizes to stock most of)
Range — how spread out values are
(temperature highs vs lows this week)
⚠️ WATCH OUT!
❌ Always sort first before finding median
❌ Range is max − min, not a single value
❌ There can be no mode (all values unique)
❌ Mean can be a decimal (that's okay!)
❌ Median for even count: average the two middles
