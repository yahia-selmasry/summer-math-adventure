What numbers can we multiply together to get 12, Max?
12 = ?
Um… 3×4? Or 2×6? Or 1×12? They're all different!
All of those are correct! Numbers that multiply to give you 12 are called FACTORS of 12. Work in pairs starting from 1!

Divisibility Rules (shortcuts!):
÷2: ends in 0, 2, 4, 6, 8
÷3: digit sum ÷ 3 evenly (126: 1+2+6=9 ✓)
÷5: ends in 0 or 5
÷10: ends in 0
÷6: divisible by BOTH 2 AND 3
÷9: digit sum ÷ 9 evenly
A PRIME number has exactly 2 factors: 1 and itself. A COMPOSITE has more!
Special: 1 is NEITHER prime nor composite!
