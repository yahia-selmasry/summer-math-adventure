Formulas to remember:
Perimeter of rectangle = 2 × (length + width)  |  Area of rectangle = length × width
Volume of rectangular prism = length × width × height
Area of triangle = 1/2 × base × height

Part A – Perimeter Problems

Draw a quick sketch. Label all sides. Add them all up for perimeter.

1. A school track is rectangular with a length of 120 meters and a width of 60 meters. How far does a runner travel in one lap around the track?

Perimeter: meters

2. Mrs. Garcia wants to put a fence around her rectangular yard. The yard is 35 feet long and 22 feet wide. Fencing costs $4.50 per foot. How much will the fence cost?

3. A square garden has a perimeter of 48 feet. What is the length of each side? What is the area of the garden?

Side length: ft   Area: sq ft

4. A picture frame has a perimeter of 72 inches. The length is twice the width. What are the dimensions of the frame?

Width: in   Length: in

Part C – Volume Problems

Volume = l × w × h. Think of stacking layers of area.

9. A fish tank is 24 inches long, 12 inches wide, and 16 inches tall. What is the volume of the tank in cubic inches?

Volume: cubic inches

10. A storage box holds 120 cubic feet of stuff. The box is 6 feet long and 5 feet wide. How tall is the box?

Height: feet

11. A shipping company has boxes that are 2 ft × 2 ft × 3 ft. How many of these boxes fit inside a truck that is 20 ft long, 8 ft wide, and 6 ft tall?

Truck volume: cu ft   Box volume: cu ft   Boxes:

12. A sandbox is 4 feet long, 3 feet wide, and 0.5 foot deep. Sand costs $8 per cubic foot. How much does it cost to fill the sandbox?

Volume: cu ft   Cost:

Part D – Missing Dimensions

Work backward! If you know area and one side, divide to find the other.

13. A rectangular swimming pool has an area of 312 square feet. If it is 24 feet long, how wide is it?

Width: feet

14. A garden has a perimeter of 56 meters. The width is 10 meters. What is the length?

Length: meters

15. A rectangular bulletin board has an area of 180 square inches. The length is 15 inches. What is the perimeter?

Width: in   Perimeter: in

16. A rectangular room needs exactly 52 meters of baseboard trim (perimeter). If the room is 15 meters long, what is the width? What is the area of the room?

Width: m   Area: sq m

Answer Key – Week 5 Word Problems: Geometry

ANSWER KEY

Page 1 Answers

1. P = 2(120+60) = 360 meters per lap
2. P = 2(35+22) = 114 ft; Cost = 114 × $4.50 = $513
3. Side = 48÷4 = 12 ft; Area = 12×12 = 144 sq ft
4. 2w + 2(2w) = 72 → 6w = 72 → w = 12 in, l = 24 in
5. Area = 18×14 = 252 sq ft; Cost = 252 × $3.50 = $882
6. Total = 24×15 = 360 sq ft; Planted = 10×8 = 80 sq ft; Not planted = 280 sq ft
7. Area = 12×9 = 108 sq ft; 108÷40 = 2.7 → 3 cans needed
8. Area = ½ × 16 × 9 = 72 sq ft
Challenge: Area = 80 + 30 = 110 sq ft; Cost = 110 × $4 = $440

Page 2 Answers

9. V = 24×12×16 = 4,608 cubic inches
10. Height = 120 ÷ (6×5) = 120 ÷ 30 = 4 feet
11. Truck = 20×8×6 = 960 cu ft; Box = 2×2×3 = 12 cu ft; 960 ÷ 12 = 80 boxes
12. V = 4×3×0.5 = 6 cu ft; Cost = 6 × $8 = $48
13. Width = 312 ÷ 24 = 13 feet
14. 2(l+10) = 56 → l+10 = 28 → l = 18 meters
15. Width = 180÷15 = 12 in; P = 2(15+12) = 54 inches
16. 2(15+w) = 52 → 15+w = 26 → w = 11 m; Area = 15×11 = 165 sq m
Challenge: Area = 20×12 + 10×8 = 240+80 = 320 sq ft; Perimeter of L-shape ≈ 20+12+10+8+10+4 = 64 ft (estimate depends on how L connects); Area cost = 320×$6.50 = $2,080; Perimeter cost = 64×$2.25 = $144; Total ≈ $2,224
