FRACTIONS
Adding/Subtracting fractions: Get a common denominator, then add/subtract the numerators.
Multiplying fractions: Multiply numerators together, multiply denominators together. Simplify.
Mixed numbers: Convert to improper fractions first, or work with whole parts and fraction parts separately.

Part A – Adding Fractions in Real Life

Find a common denominator before adding. Simplify your answer if possible.

1. A banana bread recipe calls for 23 cup of sugar and 14 cup of brown sugar. How much sugar is needed in all?

Answer: cup(s)

2. Maya walked 38 mile to the park and then 58 mile to the library. How far did she walk in total?

Answer: mile(s)

3. Carlos spent 12 hour reading and 25 hour on math homework. How long did he spend studying altogether?

Answer: hour(s)

4. A pizza is cut into 12 equal slices. Ty ate 312 and his sister ate 412. What fraction of the pizza did they eat together? Simplify.

Answer: of the pizza

Part B – Subtracting Fractions

Remember: borrow 1 whole as a fraction when subtracting mixed numbers if needed.

5. Dani had 78 of a carton of orange juice. She drank 38. How much is left?

Answer: of the carton

6. A rope is 56 meter long. Kenji cuts off 13 meter. How much rope remains?

Answer: meter

7. A pitcher holds 3 12 cups of lemonade. After pouring out 1 34 cups, how much is left?

Answer: cup(s)

8. A trail is 4 23 miles long. After hiking 2 56 miles, how far is left to go?

Answer: mile(s)

Part C – Multiplying Fractions

Multiply across: numerator × numerator, denominator × denominator. Always simplify!

9. A garden is 34 acre. A farmer plants vegetables on 23 of that section. What fraction of an acre is planted with vegetables?

Answer: acre

10. There are 24 students in class. If 38 of them play soccer, how many students play soccer?

Answer: students

11. A sandwich is cut into 8 equal pieces. Leo eats 12 of the sandwich. His dog eats 14 of what Leo left. What fraction of the whole sandwich did the dog eat?

Answer: of the whole

12. A recipe needs 2 12 cups of flour. If you want to make 12 of the recipe, how much flour is needed?

Answer: cup(s)

Part D – Mixed Numbers in Real Life

13. Marcus ran 1 34 miles on Monday, 2 12 miles on Wednesday, and 1 14 miles on Friday. How far did he run in total?

Answer: mile(s)

14. A board is 6 34 feet long. A carpenter cuts off 2 12 feet. How long is the board now?

Answer: feet

Answer: of the tank

Page 1 Answers

1. 2/3 + 1/4 = 8/12 + 3/12 = 11/12 cup

4. 3/12 + 4/12 = 7/12 of the pizza
5. 7/8 − 3/8 = 4/8 = 1/2 of the carton
6. 5/6 − 1/3 = 5/6 − 2/6 = 3/6 = 1/2 meter
7. 3½ − 1¾ = 3 2/4 − 1 3/4 = 2 6/4 − 1 3/4 = 1 3/4 cups
8. 4 2/3 − 2 5/6 = 4 4/6 − 2 5/6 = 3 10/6 − 2 5/6 = 1 5/6 miles

Page 2 Answers

9. 3/4 × 2/3 = 6/12 = 1/2 acre
10. 24 × 3/8 = 72/8 = 9 students
11. Dog ate 1/4 of 1/2 = 1/8 of the whole sandwich
12. 2 1/2 × 1/2 = 5/2 × 1/2 = 5/4 = 1 1/4 cups
13. 1 3/4 + 2 1/2 + 1 1/4 = 1 3/4 + 2 2/4 + 1 1/4 = 4 6/4 = 5 1/2 miles
14. 6 3/4 − 2 1/2 = 6 3/4 − 2 2/4 = 4 1/4 feet
